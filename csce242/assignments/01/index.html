Future Trips to The Moon

When will humans return to the lunar surface?

As of right now, NASA plans to send astronauts to orbit the lunar surface in late 2024 aboard the Orion capsule. This capsule will be launched by the Space Launch System (SLS) rocket under the mission name "Artemis II." In the following year, the plan is to send a crew of four to land on the lunar surface in late 2025. Due to unforeseen circumstances, the likelihood of achieving this timeline is low.

Why is returning to the moon so important?

The moon holds valuable scientific information that can help us understand the early history of the solar system and Earth. Studying lunar rocks and soil can provide insights into planetary formation and evolution. The return to the Moon will capture public imagination and inspire the next generation of scientists, engineers, and explorers. They provide educational opportunities and generate interest in STEM fields.

Benefits of returning to the moon:

  • Scientific Exploration
  • Techonlogical Advacements
  • International Collaboration
  • Space Sustainability
  • Education Advancements
  • Resource Potential

Artemis Missions Information

Mission Launch Date Description
Artemis I 2022 Uncrewed test flight of the Space Launch System and Orion spacecraft.
Artemis II 2024 Crewed mission to orbit the Moon using the Orion spacecraft.
Artemis III 2025 Human landing on the lunar surface with the goal of sustainable exploration.
